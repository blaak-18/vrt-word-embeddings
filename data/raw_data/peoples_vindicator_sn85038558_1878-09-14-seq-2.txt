NO50 LAW.
We understand that a large num
ber of colored men in this parish are
preaching a crusade against law.
They have been put up to it no
doubt, but we complain nevertheless
that they are most susceptible of be
ing "put up" to believe and do bad
things.
This preaching for "no law"
amounts to a violent tirade against
the courts for convicting negro thieves
and ridding honest communities of
them by putting all such in the
penitentiary. To this, the average
Radical negro objects, and he is sue
tained in the objection-onay, encour
aged in it-by Blunt and his army of
preachers. The white people don't
intend to be eternally ding-donging
into the negro what he ought to do.
He knows well enough already. Nor
do we intend to apologise for the
courts. They have done their duty
fearlessly and impartially, and we
intend to sustain their decisions. We
refer to this argument now being
used against the whites simply to
show that the negro is determined to
be a bad citizen if he can possibly do
so-and that on the lamest excuse.
He insists that he has the right to
steal without punishment; he insists
he has the right to vote for and elect
all manner of liars and felons to of
fice, and he insists there shall neither
be moral or legal restraint on him in
either of these rights--and all the
time he knotws he is doing wrong.
Where innocent people do harm
through ignorance it is all well, but
when they do it from blind hatred
and with a determination to injure,
they deserve and will receive pun
ishment.
As the negro has selected to do in
jury, not only to the material inter
eats of his employer and superior,
but is determined to suppress all law ;
as he is determined that license is
what be demands, not liberty, he will
be treated with such rigor as will give
him a lasting lesson in citizenship.
Spurning the advice and tuition of
those who would be, his. friends; de
termined to destroy, all honor and
virtue, that he may riot in barbarism,
be must take the consequences. The
white race are determined on honest
government, lie is determined on the
contrary-then let him asseme all the
responsibility of his acts. He will
have no child's play to indulge in,
and a lesson will be taught him this
year he'll not forget soon.
